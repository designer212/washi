Our Core Strategic Actions

WASH International Trust Malawi targets vulnerable community hubs, early childhood centers, and rural households to introduce localized, long-term self-supporting infrastructures.

Pillar 1

Safe Water Supply & Hygiene

Improving instant family access to clean drinking sources by establishing customized domestic and cluster pump assets built to withstand long-term usage.

Learn More
Pillar 2

Small-Scale Irrigation Systems

Equipping youth groups and community agricultural blocks with high-efficiency irrigation kits to secure robust food yields outside rain timelines.

Learn More
Pillar 3

Women Economic Empowerment

Facilitating grassroots Village Savings and Loans Associations (VSLAs) to provide marginalized rural mothers with secure micro-capital access.

Learn More
Pillar 4

Ventilated Improved Sanitation

Building proper, highly adaptive family ventilated latrines to secure baseline rural sanitation and protect local shallow groundwater from threats.

Learn More
Pillar 5

Environmental Management

Enforcing rigorous catchment area protection, stabilizing local soil structures, and conducting ecosystem mapping to guard critical local aquifers.

Learn More
Pillar 6

Green Villages Campaign

Enforcing active community tree placement via regional seed banks, diverse agro-forestry actions, and sustainable honey bee farming assets.

Learn More
Pillar 7

Geospatial Info & Mapping

Leveraging remote sensing platforms and precise digital data tracking setups to safely monitor physical asset lifespans and identify infrastructure gaps.

Learn More

Administrative Stewardship

Operating under strict internal controls and comprehensive field audits to ensure development capital directly creates functional infrastructure assets.

Data-Driven Mapping

Utilizing active geospatial assessments to eliminate redundancies and optimize deployment intervals across high-vulnerability targets.

Grassroots Ownership

All implemented water, agricultural, and sanitation systems are fully transferred to native community committees for localized operational continuity.

Support Our Community Impact

We actively collaborate towards the global agenda of ending deep structural WASH poverty by expanding community self-supporting systems. We welcome international grantors, bilateral partners, local consortiums, and technical foundations to back our low-cost, adaptive asset installations across rural locations in Malawi.
